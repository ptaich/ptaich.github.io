CLEVELAND MUSEUM OF ART
SHINTO:
DISCOVERY OF THE DIVINE IN JAPANESE ART
Tue, 04/09/2019 to Sun, 06/30/2019
The Kelvin and Eleanor Smith Foundation Exhibition Hall
Admission: $15 • $8 with Ohio ID

The veneration of deities called kami has been a central feature of Japanese culture for many centuries and the inspiration for a wide range of Japanese visual art. Most recently, kami veneration falls under the auspices of a diverse body of rites known as Shinto, or the “Way of the Gods.”

The first exploration devoted to Shinto art from collections in both the United States and Japan, this exhibition introduces works exemplifying kami worship from the Heian period (794–1185) through the Edo period (1615–1868).
Shinto: Discovery of the Divine in Japanese Art is organized by the Cleveland Museum of Art with the special cooperation of the Nara National Museum.
